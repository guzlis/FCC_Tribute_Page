A tribute to Black Cats

Because they are just as loveable as any other cat

Cool facts about black cats

  • There are 22 breeds of black cats
  • Genetically, black is the most dominant fur color
  • October 27th is National Black Cat Day
  • The idea of black cats being a sign of bad luck is a very central european one.
  • In Japan, it is believed that a black cat can increase your chances at finding the love of your life
  • In England, it is said that a bride-to-be who is gifted a black cat will have a happy marriage
  • Further north, the Scots believe that a black cat is a sign of future prosperity
  • British sailors believed that a black cat could also ensure their safe return to shore. For this reason, fishermen's wives kept black cats in their home.
  • The myth about cats being bad luck goes back all the way to ancient greece.
  • According to myth, Hecate, the goddess of witchcraft, turned one of her servants into a black cat (personally, I wouldn't really call that a "punishment", but hey, what do I know). Said servant remained loyal and aided Hecate in her witchcraft adventures
  • In the year 1233, Pope Gregory XI declared black cats as symbols of Satan, and thus the mistreatment of black cats began
  • In 1961, over 150 black cats auditioned for the starring role in the film adaptation of Edgar Allan Poe's "The Black Cat"
  • Black cats are notoriously difficult to photograph
  • Because of the stigma, black cats are the least likely to be adopted, and the most likely to be euthanized in a shelter
